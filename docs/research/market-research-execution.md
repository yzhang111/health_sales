# Market Research Execution Plan

## Decision Goal

Determine whether the nutrition assistant can deliver clear business value for sales reps while staying compliant and clinically conservative.

## Research Questions

1. What customer problems are most frequent and high-value for reps?
2. What recommendation depth is useful without crossing medical boundaries?
3. What competitor patterns exist in report quality and compliance messaging?
4. What evidence quality threshold is acceptable for each recommendation class?
5. What metrics correlate with better rep conversion and customer retention?

## Evidence Standards

- Every key claim must include source, publish date, and context.
- Label each statement as:
  - `Fact`
  - `Inference`
  - `Recommendation`
- Flag data older than 24 months as potentially stale.
- Include at least one contrarian finding per major conclusion.

## Research Tracks

### Track A: Customer Problem Frequency

- Inputs: rep interviews, CRM notes, historical consultation logs.
- Output: ranked problem list (sleep, recovery, fatigue, etc.) with frequency and urgency.

### Track B: Competitive Benchmark

- Inputs: product demos, public docs, pricing pages, user reviews.
- Output: competitor matrix with feature depth, compliance posture, and gaps.

### Track C: Evidence Mapping

- Inputs: guidelines, review papers, trusted health organizations, formularies.
- Output: claim-to-evidence map by scenario and customer segment.

### Track D: Commercial Feasibility

- Inputs: pilot assumptions, conversion funnel, follow-up cadence.
- Output: value model with expected rep productivity and adoption lift.

## Deliverable Format

1. Executive summary
2. Key findings
3. Implications for product decisions
4. Risks and caveats
5. Recommendation
6. Source list

## Output Gate

- No unsourced numeric claims.
- All assumptions explicit.
- Recommendation directly tied to evidence.
- Clear decision statement: proceed, proceed-with-constraints, or hold.
